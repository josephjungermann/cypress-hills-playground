Cypress Hills Playground Renovation Provides Brooklyn Residents Hope for a Brighter Future

East New York residents joined the NYC Department of Parks and Recreation Brooklyn Commissioner Martin Maher (third from left), Council Member Inez Barron (center) and Assembly member Charles Barron (center) on Friday, Nov. 15, for a ribbon-cutting ceremony to reveal renovations to Cypress Hills Playground on Euclid Avenue, Brooklyn, N.Y. (Joseph Jungermann/NYCity News Service)
By Joseph Jungermann (@j_mann29)

Friday morning was not just another walk in the park for East New York residents.

After nearly a year of construction, the Department of Parks and Recreation and Brooklyn City Council and community board members unveiled a freshly renovated Cypress Hills playground and park.

“This is a totally new park. Our community is entitled to this; our community deserves this,” said Council Member Inez Barron (D-Brooklyn) standing on the park’s new multi-purpose athletic field made from high-quality synthetic turf.

The $6 million city-funded project comes nearly two decades after the last renovation of the site.

The upgrade includes not just a flood-proof turf field with two baseball diamonds and markings for football and soccer fields, but also a brand-new recreational track, reconstructed bleachers and two dugouts in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act.

“I’m so happy. This is very enlightening, and I’m happy to see the change,” said Adeline Bunche, 90, a long-time Brooklyn resident and member of Operation P.O.W.E.R., an East New York organization that advocates for human rights and equality.

Bunche, who has been a Brooklyn resident for 53 years, reflected on what the rebuild of Cypress Hills Playground and park meant to her, but mostly, the future generation that includes her great- great grandson.

“I’m glad they have a track. The kids need a park like this they can use every single day.”

Just two years ago, the field inside the park consisted of only a baseball and softball field. No track existed, the playing field’s clay was eroding, and puddles of water would pool on the infield that was surrounded by a badly weathered concrete grandstand.

“I inherited this project and the field was so bad,” said Svetlana Filipovich, a consultant project manager for the parks department.

The area was missing simple necessities that a lot of today’s public areas have.

“When I presented the project to the community board, people were saying there was no lighting. People asked for light,” said Filipovich.

The playground park now has new, energy-efficient LED lights surrounding the track so residents can exercise at night.

The design team also included multi-faucet water fountains at child and adult heights, misting stations to keep residents cool on hot days and rodent-proof garbage cans.

“I’m thrilled to see these improvements come through,” said, Martin Maher, Brooklyn commissioner of the Parks Department.

Maher, who has dedicated himself to revamping parks all over Brooklyn and is currently seeking bids on a $10 million construction project for Brownsville Recreation Center on Linden Boulevard, led the Cypress Hills Playground ribbon-cutting ceremony on Friday afternoon.

“This park is going to be here for a long time,” he told a proud East New York crowd.

The playground aims to uplift neighborhood spirit, Barron said. “Parks are one of the great equalizers of our community.”
